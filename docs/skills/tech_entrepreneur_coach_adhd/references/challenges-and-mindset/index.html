Common Challenges & Mindset

Challenge 1: "I Don't Know What to Build"‚Äã

Big Tech Mindset: "We need user research, market analysis, competitive landscape..."

Indie Reality:

Build something YOU need.
Ex-FAANG engineers have problems worth solving:
- Tools for managing ADHD productivity
- ML workflow automation
- Code review assistants
- Time tracking for consultants
- Portfolio/resume builders for tech
- Interview prep platforms

You are the target audience.
Build for yourself, charge others like you.

Challenge 2: "I Can't Do Marketing/Sales"‚Äã

Big Tech Mindset: "We have marketing teams for this"

Indie Reality:

You don't need to "do marketing."
You need to have conversations.

Script:
"I built [thing] because I had [problem].
 Does this resonate with you?"

If yes: "Want to try it?"
If no: "What would make it useful?"

That's it. Repeat 100 times.

ADHD Hack: Frame it as "user research" not "sales"

  • Your brain likes solving problems (research)
  • Your brain hates "selling" (feels icky) ‚Üí Reframe and it becomes doable

Challenge 3: "I Keep Starting New Projects"‚Äã

This is the #1 ADHD killer of indie success.

The Rule: ONE project in "launch" phase at a time.

Allowed:

  • ‚úÖ Maintain existing profitable app
  • ‚úÖ Work on ONE new app
  • ‚úÖ Take notes on future ideas

Not allowed:

  • ‚ùå Work on multiple pre-launch projects
  • ‚ùå Rebuild existing app from scratch
  • ‚ùå Start "just a quick experiment" (lies)

Use "Idea Backlog" (Notion):

  • When new idea hits: Write it down
  • Set reminder: Review in 1 month
  • Most ideas won't survive 30 days
  • The ones that do = worth building

Challenge 4: "I'm Running Out of Money"‚Äã

Runway Planning (Critical)

Calculate:

  • Monthly expenses: $X
  • Current savings: $Y
  • Runway: Y / X months

If runway < 6 months: URGENT: Get to first dollar FAST

  • Simplify MVP (1 week to launch)
  • Freelance 2 days/week ($200-300/hour ML consulting)
  • Live on minimal expenses

If runway 6-12 months: FOCUSED: Execute the plan

  • 2-week MVP
  • 1 month to $1K MRR
  • 3 months to $5K MRR

If runway > 12 months: STRATEGIC: Take time to validate

  • Test multiple ideas
  • Build audience first
  • Choose best opportunity

Don't let runway hit zero. At 3 months remaining: Get freelance income or pivot.

Challenge 5: "Impostor Syndrome"‚Äã

Big Tech Mindset: "I'm just a Google engineer, why would anyone buy from me?"

Reality Check - You have MASSIVE advantages:

  1. Technical Depth Most indie hackers can't build what you can. Your "basic" = someone else's "impossible"

  2. Big Tech Credential "Ex-Google ML Engineer" = instant credibility Use it in bio, landing page, marketing

  3. Problem-Solving You debugged distributed systems at scale. A small business problem? Easy mode.

  4. AI Native You understand LLMs better than 99% of people. This is unfair advantage in 2024+

Your impostor syndrome is lying. You're more qualified than you think.

ADHD + Entrepreneurship: Honest Assessment‚Äã

What Makes It Harder‚Äã

  • ‚ùå No external structure (meetings, deadlines)
  • ‚ùå No team to catch your mistakes
  • ‚ùå Decision fatigue (you decide EVERYTHING)
  • ‚ùå Inconsistent income (ADHD hates uncertainty)
  • ‚ùå Easy to doom-scroll instead of work
  • ‚ùå Rejection sensitivity (product criticism hurts)

What Makes It Easier‚Äã

  • ‚úÖ Work when hyperfocus strikes (2am? Fine!)
  • ‚úÖ No boring meetings
  • ‚úÖ Only work on interesting problems
  • ‚úÖ Direct feedback (revenue = validation)
  • ‚úÖ Dopamine from shipping + earning
  • ‚úÖ Control your environment completely

The Honest Assessment‚Äã

Entrepreneurship is NOT for everyone with ADHD.

You'll thrive if:
‚úì You have high frustration tolerance
‚úì You can handle rejection
‚úì You're okay with uncertainty
‚úì You have support system (partner, friends)
‚úì You're willing to do boring tasks sometimes
‚úì You can recognize when to quit

You'll struggle if:
‚úó You need external validation constantly
‚úó You can't handle financial stress
‚úó You abandon projects at first obstacle
‚úó You won't do any marketing/sales
‚úó You need immediate results (patience required)

Be honest with yourself.
There's no shame in going back to W2.

Emergency Protocols‚Äã

When You're Stuck (The 3-Day Rule)‚Äã

Day 1: Acknowledge you're stuck. Don't shame yourself.

Day 2: Talk to someone

  • DM another indie hacker
  • Post in community: "I'm stuck on X. Help?"

Day 3: Change something

  • New location (coffee shop)
  • Different task (skip to easier thing)
  • Ask for help (hire contractor for $100 to unblock)

If still stuck after 3 days: ‚Üí The scope is too big. Cut features. ‚Üí Or the idea isn't exciting anymore. Okay to pivot.

When You Want to Quit‚Äã

Before quitting, ask:

  1. Is this a bad idea or am I just tired? (If tired: Take a week off. Revisit after.)

  2. Have I given it a fair shot? (Fair shot = 3 months of consistent effort)

  3. Is there any traction? (Even 5 happy users = signal)

  4. What would I do instead? (If answer is "go back to FAANG": Maybe take a break first)

It's okay to quit. Sunk cost fallacy is real. But make sure you're quitting the right thing for the right reasons.
